froot — execution architecture

How the durable Temporal workflows and their activities fan out, and the through-line of PRs #16–#19: a single loop registry the spine, the dashboard, and the starter all read instead of branching on the loop.

1 · Workflows and their activity calls

Every box that says Workflow is durable and replayable; every leaf is an @activity.defn (all the I/O and nondeterminism lives there). Edges read top-to-bottom in execution order — the labels are the logic. Two families: the acting loops propose a PR a gate merges or reverts; the advisory loops scan open PRs and upsert one decaying comment, never a merge.

2 · The rework (PRs #16–#19): one registry, not N branches

The big picture. Before, the loop enum was a key every consumer re-interpreted with its own per-loop branching — and the dashboard and starter carried twin copies per family. After, each loop self-registers one LoopSpec (a disposition-tagged union), and the spine, the dashboard, and a single starter all read the registry.

Beforebranch on the loop, everywhere

Per-loop knowledge is copied across the spine, the dashboard (two near-identical families), and three starter modules.

Afterregister once, read the registry

One spec per loop; the disposition (commit-or-revert vs emit-signal) is derived from the tail's type. Adding a loop is a registration, not an edit across the codebase.
